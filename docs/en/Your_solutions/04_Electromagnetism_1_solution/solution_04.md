# Force Comparison: Electric vs. Gravitational Force

## Problem Statement
Calculate the magnitude of the electric force and the gravitational force between an electron and a proton in a hydrogen atom (average distance $r \approx 5.3 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$). What is the ratio $F_e/F_g$?

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: List the Universal Constants
To solve this, we need the standard physical constants for an electron and a proton:
* **Distance ($r$):** $5.3 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$
* **Elementary Charge ($e$):** $1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Mass of Electron ($m_e$):** $9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
* **Mass of Proton ($m_p$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* **Coulomb's Constant ($k$):** $8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$
* **Gravitational Constant ($G$):** $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

### Step 2: Calculate Electric Force ($F_e$)
Use Coulomb's Law. Since the electron and proton have the same magnitude of charge ($e$), $q_1 = q_2 = e$.
$$F_e = k \frac{|q_1 \cdot q_2|}{r^2} = k \frac{e^2}{r^2}$$
$$F_e = (8.99 \times 10^9) \frac{(1.60 \times 10^{-19})^2}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_e = (8.99 \times 10^9) \frac{2.56 \times 10^{-38}}{2.809 \times 10^{-21}}$$
**$$F_e \approx 8.2 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$$**

### Step 3: Calculate Gravitational Force ($F_g$)
Use Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation.
$$F_g = G \frac{m_e \cdot m_p}{r^2}$$
$$F_g = (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(1.67 \times 10^{-27})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_g = (6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \frac{1.52 \times 10^{-57}}{2.809 \times 10^{-21}}$$
**$$F_g \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$$**

### Step 4: Calculate the Ratio ($F_e / F_g$)
Now, divide the electric force by the gravitational force to see which one is stronger, and by how much.
$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{8.2 \times 10^{-8}}{3.6 \times 10^{-47}}$$
**$$\text{Ratio} \approx 2.3 \times 10^{39}$$**

---
## Conclusion (The Physics "Cheat Code")
The ratio is $2.3 \times 10^{39}$. This means the electric force holding the atom together is **2.3 undecillion times stronger** than the gravitational force between them! Because of this massive difference, **we completely ignore gravity when calculating atomic physics.**
