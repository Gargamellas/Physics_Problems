# Electric Field: Levitating a Proton

## Problem Statement
What electric field strength is required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity at the level of the Earth's surface? 
* $m_p \approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* $e \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Decode the Keyword
The keyword is **"levitate"**. This means the proton is in equilibrium in the air. The net force is zero. Therefore, the downward gravitational force must be perfectly balanced by the upward electric force.
$$F_{up} = F_{down}$$
$$F_e = F_g$$

### Step 2: Write the Force Equations
* **Gravitational Force ($F_g$):** $m_p \cdot g$
* **Electric Force ($F_e$):** $q \cdot E$ (where $q$ is the proton's charge, $e$)

Substitute these into our balance equation:
$$e \cdot E = m_p \cdot g$$

### Step 3: Solve for Electric Field ($E$)
Rearrange the formula to isolate $E$:
$$E = \frac{m_p \cdot g}{e}$$

### Step 4: Plug in the Constants
$$E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}) \cdot (9.8 \text{ m/s}^2)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}}$$
$$E = \frac{16.366 \times 10^{-27}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$E \approx 10.228 \times 10^{-8}$$

To write this in proper scientific notation:
**$$E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$$** (or V/m)

**Final Answer:**
To make the proton levitate, an electric field strength of approximately **$1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$** is required. Since the proton is positively charged, this electric field must point **upward** to push the proton up against gravity.
