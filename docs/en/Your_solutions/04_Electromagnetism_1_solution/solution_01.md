# Coulomb's Law: Force at the Center of a Square

## Step 1: Variables
* $q_1 = q_2 = q_3 = q_4 = +1.0 \text{ C}$
* $q_{center} = -2.0 \text{ C}$
* $a = 1.0 \text{ m}$

## Step 2: Distance
$$r = \frac{a \sqrt{2}}{2}$$
$$r = \frac{1.0 \sqrt{2}}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \text{ m}$$

## Step 3: Magnitudes
$$F = k \frac{|q \cdot q_{center}|}{r^2}$$
$$F = k \frac{(1.0)(2.0)}{(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2})^2}$$
$$F = k \frac{2.0}{0.5} = 4k \text{ N}$$

## Step 4: Vectors
* $\vec{F}_1 + \vec{F}_3 = 0$
* $\vec{F}_2 + \vec{F}_4 = 0$

$$\vec{F}_{net} = \vec{F}_1 + \vec{F}_2 + \vec{F}_3 + \vec{F}_4 = 0 \text{ N}$$

**Result:**
* **Magnitude:** 0 N
* **Direction:** None
