# Lorentz Force on a Current-Carrying Wire

## Problem Statement
A straight wire 2.0 m long carries a current of 10 A. It is placed in a uniform magnetic field of B = 0.5 T. Calculate the magnetic force on the wire if the angle between the wire and the magnetic field is:
a) $90^\circ$
b) $45^\circ$
c) $0^\circ$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Identify the Variables and the Formula
The magnetic force on a current-carrying wire is given by the formula:
**$$F = I \cdot L \cdot B \cdot \sin(\theta)$$**

Where:
* **Current ($I$):** $10 \text{ A}$
* **Length ($L$):** $2.0 \text{ m}$
* **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.5 \text{ T}$
* **Angle ($\theta$):** Varies per section.

First, let's calculate the constant part of our equation ($I \cdot L \cdot B$):
$$I \cdot L \cdot B = 10 \cdot 2.0 \cdot 0.5 = 10 \text{ N}$$

Now, the formula simplifies to: 
$$F = 10 \cdot \sin(\theta)$$

### Step 2: Calculate for Each Angle

**a) Angle is $90^\circ$ (Perpendicular):**
$$F = 10 \cdot \sin(90^\circ)$$
Since $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$:
**$$F = 10 \cdot 1 = 10 \text{ N}$$**
*(This is the maximum possible force).*

**b) Angle is $45^\circ$:**
$$F = 10 \cdot \sin(45^\circ)$$
Since $\sin(45^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707$:
$$F = 10 \cdot 0.707$$
**$$F \approx 7.07 \text{ N}$$**

**c) Angle is $0^\circ$ (Parallel):**
$$F = 10 \cdot \sin(0^\circ)$$
Since $\sin(0^\circ) = 0$:
**$$F = 10 \cdot 0 = 0 \text{ N}$$**
*(Physics Cheat Code: When a wire runs perfectly parallel to the magnetic field lines, it experiences absolutely zero magnetic force).*
