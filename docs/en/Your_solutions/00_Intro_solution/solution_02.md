# Systems of Linear Equations: Solution

This document demonstrates how to solve a system of two linear equations with two variables using the **Substitution Method**.

## Problem Statement

Find the values of $x$ and $y$ that satisfy the following system:

1.  $2x + 3y = 12$
2.  $x - y = 1$

---

## Step 1: Isolate one variable

We will start with the second equation ($x - y = 1$) because it is simpler to rearrange. We isolate $x$ by adding $y$ to both sides:

$$
x = y + 1
$$



---

## Step 2: Substitute into the first equation

Now, we take the expression for $x$ (which is $y + 1$) and substitute it into the first equation ($2x + 3y = 12$):

$$
2(y + 1) + 3y = 12
$$

---

## Step 3: Solve for $y$

Distribute the $2$ and combine like terms:

$$
2y + 2 + 3y = 12
$$

$$
5y + 2 = 12
$$

Subtract $2$ from both sides:

$$
5y = 10
$$

Divide by $5$:

$$
y = 2
$$

---

## Step 4: Solve for $x$

Now that we know $y = 2$, substitute this value back into our isolated equation from Step 1 ($x = y + 1$):

$$
x = 2 + 1
$$

$$
x = 3
$$

---

## Final Solution

The values that satisfy both equations are:

$$
x = 3, \quad y = 2
$$

### Verification

To double-check, plug the values into the original equations:
* $2(3) + 3(2) = 6 + 6 = 12$ (Correct)
* $3 - 2 = 1$ (Correct)
