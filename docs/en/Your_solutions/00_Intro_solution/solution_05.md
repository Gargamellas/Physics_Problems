# Vector Decomposition: Trigonometry in 2D Space

This document demonstrates how to resolve a vector into its horizontal ($x$) and vertical ($y$) components using trigonometric functions.

## Problem Statement

A vector $\vec{A}$ is defined by:
- **Magnitude:** $|\vec{A}| = 15$
- **Angle:** $\theta = 60^\circ$ (with the horizontal axis)



Find the horizontal component ($A_x$) and the vertical component ($A_y$).

---

## Step 1: Use Trigonometric Identities

In a right-angled triangle formed by the vector and its components:
* The **horizontal component** is the adjacent side: $\cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$
* The **vertical component** is the opposite side: $\sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$

Therefore, we use the following formulas:

$$
A_x = |\vec{A}| \cos(\theta)
$$

$$
A_y = |\vec{A}| \sin(\theta)
$$

---

## Step 2: Calculate the Horizontal Component ($A_x$)

Substitute the given values into the cosine formula:

$$
A_x = 15 \times \cos(60^\circ)
$$

Since $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$:

$$
A_x = 15 \times 0.5 = 7.5
$$

---

## Step 3: Calculate the Vertical Component ($A_y$)

Substitute the given values into the sine formula:

$$
A_y = 15 \times \sin(60^\circ)
$$

Since $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$:

$$
A_y = 15 \times 0.866 \approx 12.99
$$

---

## Final Solution

The components of vector $\vec{A}$ are:

$$
\vec{A} = [7.5, 12.99]
$$

### Summary
* **Horizontal ($A_x$):** $7.5$
* **Vertical ($A_y$):** $12.99$
