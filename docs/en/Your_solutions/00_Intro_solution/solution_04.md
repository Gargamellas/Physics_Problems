# Rearranging Formulas: The Simple Pendulum

This document demonstrates how to rearrange the formula for the period of a simple pendulum to solve for the acceleration due to gravity ($g$).

## Problem Statement

The formula for the period ($T$) of a simple pendulum is:

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

[Image of simple pendulum formula and components]

Where:
* $T$ = Period of the pendulum
* $L$ = Length of the pendulum
* $g$ = Acceleration due to gravity
* $\pi$ = Mathematical constant (pi)

**Goal:** Rearrange the equation to isolate $g$.

---

## Step 1: Divide by $2\pi$

First, we move the constant $2\pi$ to the other side by dividing both sides of the equation:

$$
\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

---

## Step 2: Square both sides

To remove the square root, we square both sides of the equation:

$$
\left( \frac{T}{2\pi} \right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}
$$

Expanding the left side:

$$
\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}
$$

---

## Step 3: Invert the fractions (or Cross-Multiply)

We want $g$ in the numerator. We can take the reciprocal of both sides:

$$
\frac{4\pi^2}{T^2} = \frac{g}{L}
$$

---

## Step 4: Isolate $g$

Finally, multiply both sides by $L$ to solve for $g$:

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$

---

## Final Result

The rearranged formula to calculate the acceleration due to gravity is:

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$

### Summary of Operations
1. **Divide** by $2\pi$.
2. **Square** both sides.
3. **Isolate** $g$ through algebraic multiplication/division.
