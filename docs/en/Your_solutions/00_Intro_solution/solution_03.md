# Proportionality in Physics: Universal Law of Gravitation

This document demonstrates how the gravitational force between two objects changes when their masses and the distance between them are modified.

## Problem Statement

According to the Universal Law of Gravitation:

$$
F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$



[Image of universal law of gravitation formula and diagram]


Determine the factor by which the force $F$ changes if:
1. The distance $r$ is **doubled** ($r' = 2r$).
2. Both masses $m_1$ and $m_2$ are **halved** ($m_1' = \frac{1}{2}m_1$ and $m_2' = \frac{1}{2}m_2$).

---

## Step 1: Define the Initial State

Let the initial force be $F_1$:

$$
F_1 = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

---

## Step 2: Define the New State

We substitute the new values into the original formula to find the new force $F_2$:

* $m_1 \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}m_1$
* $m_2 \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}m_2$
* $r \rightarrow 2r$

$$
F_2 = G \frac{(\frac{1}{2}m_1)(\frac{1}{2}m_2)}{(2r)^2}
$$

---

## Step 3: Simplify the Expression

First, let's simplify the numerator:

$$
(\frac{1}{2}m_1)(\frac{1}{2}m_2) = \frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2
$$

Next, simplify the denominator:

$$
(2r)^2 = 4r^2
$$

Now, substitute these back into the equation for $F_2$:

$$
F_2 = G \frac{\frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2}{4r^2}
$$

---

## Step 4: Calculate the Change Factor

To see the relationship between $F_2$ and $F_1$, we pull the constants to the front:

$$
F_2 = \frac{1/4}{4} \cdot \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right)
$$

Since $\frac{1}{4} \div 4 = \frac{1}{16}$:

$$
F_2 = \frac{1}{16} F_1
$$

---

## Final Conclusion

If the distance is doubled and both masses are halved, the gravitational force decreases by a factor of **16**.

$$
F_{new} = \frac{1}{16} F_{old}
$$
