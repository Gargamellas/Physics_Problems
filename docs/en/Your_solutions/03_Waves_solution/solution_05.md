# Sound Waves: Calculating Distance from an Echo

## Problem Statement
A person shouts towards a cliff and hears the echo **1 seconds** later. How far away is the cliff? (Speed of sound in air is **343 m/s**).

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

This is a classic kinematics problem involving the reflection of sound. 

### Step 1: Account for the Echo
The key word is **"echo"**. An echo means the sound traveled to the cliff AND bounced back to the person. 
Therefore, the **1 second** total time is for a round trip. To find the distance to the cliff, we only need the time it took for the sound to reach the cliff (one way).

* $t_{total} = 1 \text{ s}$
* $t_{one-way} = \frac{1}{2} = 0.5 \text{ s}$

### Step 2: Calculate the Distance
Now, use the basic constant velocity formula ($Distance = Speed \times Time$):
$$x = v \cdot t$$

Substitute the known values:
* **Speed ($v$):** 343 m/s
* **Time ($t$):** 0.5 s

$$x = 343 \cdot 0.5$$
$$x = 171.5$$

**Final Answer:**
The cliff is **171.5 meters** away.
