# Waves: Calculating Phase Difference

## Problem Statement
What is the phase difference in radians between two points on a wave that are separated by a distance of $\lambda/3$?

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

This problem is a direct application of the phase difference formula. 

### Step 1: Identify the Formula
Whenever you see the keywords **"phase difference"** and a given **"distance"**, use the following template formula:
$$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \Delta x$$

Where:
* **$\Delta \phi$** = Phase difference (what we want to find)
* **$\lambda$** = Wavelength
* **$\Delta x$** = Distance between the two points

### Step 2: Plug in the Given Values
The problem states that the distance ($\Delta x$) is **$\lambda/3$**. Substitute this into the formula:
$$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \frac{\lambda}{3}$$

### Step 3: Simplify the Equation
Now, perform the math. Since we are multiplying fractions, the $\lambda$ in the numerator (top) and the $\lambda$ in the denominator (bottom) cancel each other out perfectly:
$$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi \cdot \lambda}{3 \cdot \lambda}$$
$$\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3}$$

**Final Answer:**
The phase difference between the two points is **$2\pi/3$ radians**.
