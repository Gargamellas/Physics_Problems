# Wave Properties: Wavelength in Air vs. Water

## Problem Statement
A sound wave in air has a frequency of **440 Hz**. If the speed of sound in air is **343 m/s**, what is its wavelength? What is its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is **1482 m/s**?

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

The fundamental relationship between wave speed ($v$), frequency ($f$), and wavelength ($\lambda$) is given by the wave equation:
$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$

To find the wavelength, we rearrange the formula to isolate $\lambda$:
$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$

> **Key Concept:** When a wave passes from one medium to another (e.g., from air to water), its speed and wavelength change, but its **frequency remains constant**. Therefore, $f = 440 \text{ Hz}$ for both calculations.

### Step 1: Calculate Wavelength in Air
* **Speed ($v$):** 343 m/s
* **Frequency ($f$):** 440 Hz

$$\lambda_{air} = \frac{343}{440}$$
$$\lambda_{air} \approx 0.7795$$

**Result 1:** The wavelength of the sound wave in air is approximately **0.78 meters**.

### Step 2: Calculate Wavelength in Water
* **Speed ($v$):** 1482 m/s
* **Frequency ($f$):** 440 Hz

$$\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482}{440}$$
$$\lambda_{water} \approx 3.3681$$

**Result 2:** The wavelength of the sound wave in water is approximately **3.37 meters**.
